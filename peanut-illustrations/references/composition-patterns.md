# Composition Patterns & Originality Rules

## Base structure types

Pick one structure; do not mix too many.

### Workflow

For: input -> process -> output, content production, AI workflows, automation chains.

How: input on the left, the peanut or a strange machine processing in the middle, output on the right, one warm-orange arrow for the main flow.

### System slice

For: information sources, filters, databases, renderers, a slice of an agent system.

How: draw only 3-5 core modules; the peanut performs one key action among them.

### Before / after

For: chaotic vs. ordered, manual vs. automatic, scattered vs. gathered, anxious vs. calm.

How: chaos on the left, calm on the right, one warm-orange arrow between them. The peanut can be more exaggerated.

### Character state

For: user pain, creator state, too many tools, information overload, stuck → moving.

How: 2-4 small states, each with one short label.

### Concept metaphor

For: a content factory, an information warehouse, a mental black box, a workflow machine, an automated report.

How: one big strange object or machine, a little input, one output. Make it memorable.

### Layered method

For: a methodology framework, system levels, a capability stack.

How: stacked boxes (not a formal pyramid); the peanut beside them hauling bricks or building.

### Map / route

For: idea → launch, a user journey, a handoff path, a learning path.

How: one winding path, a few nodes, the peanut leading the thread or walking it.

### Mini comic

For: failure → success, the real process, a gripe, before/after of using something.

How: 2-4 small panels, each panel showing exactly one action.

## How to invent a fresh metaphor

Invent a new metaphor from the current prompt every time. Do not copy a metaphor from a previous image.

### Three steps

1. Turn the abstract idea into a physical action: stuck, leaking, getting heavier, sorting, settling, fermenting, opening a door, folding, unpacking, flowing back.
2. Turn the system structure into one low-tech object: a broken machine, a cardboard box, a drawer, a pipe, a mailbox, a weird dial, a scale, a well, a ladder, an odd workstation.
3. Give the peanut the action: not standing beside it, but stuck inside the machine, pulling the wrong line, guarding a gate, hauling, patching, weighing, holding the ladder, recording, stuffing something into a strange device.

### Object pool

- Cardboard box, drawer, old machine, funnel, scale, mailbox, door, well, ladder, pipe, ball of string, gate, turntable, black box, hole punch, pasta press, clothesline, odd workstation.
- Use only 1-2 at a time; do not crowd the canvas.

### Peanut action pool

- Pull, carry, stuff, scoop, press, weigh, sew, cut, twist, guard, push, catch, unpack, label, recycle.
- The action must serve the core idea — never weird just to be weird.

## Anti-cliché rules

Every prompt deserves its own metaphor. Even for similar themes, change the metaphor — e.g. a "handoff path" need not be a road; it could be the peanut hooking a content tail onto a doorknob. "One thing, many uses" need not be a fish; it could be the peanut pressing one lump of dough into several shapes.

Do not settle into a house style of repeated props. The point of the peanut is consistency of *character and discipline*, not repetition of *scenes*.
