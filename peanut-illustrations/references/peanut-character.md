# The Peanut

## Character definition

The peanut is the recurring visual figure of this skill.

By default the peanut appears in every image. It is not a mascot, not a sticker, not cute decoration — it is a deadpan worker, gravely and calmly doing a slightly ridiculous job inside the system being drawn.

## Form

- A whole, in-shell peanut: the pinched figure-8 / double-bump silhouette.
- Hand-drawn cross-hatch shell texture (a few strokes, not dense).
- Small white dot eyes, no pupils dramatized.
- Two thin legs; occasionally two thin arms when the action needs hands.
- Outline slightly irregular and hand-drawn, never a perfect vector shape.
- Expression blank, calm, deadpan, serious.

## Personality

- Completely unbothered. Zen, even when surrounded by chaos.
- Very serious about a job that is mildly absurd.
- Like a low-key system operator who clocked in and is just doing the work.
- Dry humor; never tries to be cute.
- A little clumsy, but not dumb.
- Looks like it genuinely owns some task inside the whiteboard sketch.

## Common jobs

Give the peanut the core action:

- Hauling material that is too big for it.
- Pulling lines together to gather sources.
- Stuck inside a break in a belt or pipe.
- Operating a "judgment" lever inside a machine.
- Becoming a funnel / sorter itself.
- Cracking something open and dividing it.
- Stamping, sealing, or assembling.
- Leading a path or thread along.
- Holding up a warning sign over a pit.
- Reaching out of a hole but unable to catch what comes.
- Carrying bricks, building a bridge, opening a door, sorting, recording.

## Don't

- Do not draw the peanut as an over-cute mascot.
- Do not make it a children's-cartoon character.
- Do not give it elaborate clothing, emoji faces, or sparkly eyes.
- Do not let it just stand in a corner and watch.
- Do not let it upstage the structure it is explaining.
- Do not make it too commercial, too rounded, too polished.

## The test

If you remove the peanut and the core metaphor of the image still fully stands on its own, the peanut is too decorative. Rewrite the prompt so the peanut is the one performing the action.
